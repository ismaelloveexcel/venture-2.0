# Sales Script Pack (D) — Reply to Close

**Purpose:** Convert cold replies → discovery calls → pilot closes ($2.5k, 30-day).

**Scope:** All conversation scenarios from first reply through contract signature.

---

## 1. Reply Handling (immediate response)

### Scenario A: Positive reply ("Interesting, tell me more")

**Your response (2-3 lines, no essay):**

> Got it — quick context so you can judge fit. We identify accounts in your ICP showing active buying signals, then generate structured outreach based on why those signals matter right now. For teams like yours, we typically see 2–5 qualified conversations per batch of ~20 accounts. Worth a quick 15-min call to see if this matches your workflow? [calendar link]

**Key elements:**
- Proof (numbers, not adjectives)
- Qualification (we show relevance to their world, not generic value)
- Low friction (15 min, not "let's chat")
- Clear next step (calendar link)

---

### Scenario B: Lukewarm reply ("Sounds interesting, but send info first")

**Your response:**

> Happy to—though I find a 10-min call is faster than emails. I can show you 2–3 real accounts we flagged in your space this week and you'll know immediately if this fits. Prefer Tuesday/Thursday afternoons? [calendar link]

**Key elements:**
- Don't comply with "send info"—this stalls momentum
- Counter with a higher-value ask (seeing real data)
- Offer specific availability (reduces friction)

---

### Scenario C: Skeptical reply ("How is this different from Apollo?")

**Your response:**

> Good question. Apollo scores potential leads. We specifically surface accounts showing **active** buying window signals (funding, hiring, tech change, etc.) in the last 30 days, then generate outreach tied to that trigger. Two very different things. I can show you examples in a 15-min call—you'll see the difference. [calendar link]

**Key elements:**
- Acknowledge the question (don't dodge)
- One clear differentiator (timing + trigger specificity)
- Proof, not claims (examples in call)

---

### Scenario D: Objection: "We already use [vendor]"

**Your response:**

> Got it. Most teams still spend 40% of time researching prospects, 60% on outreach. This is about cutting that research time. If your current vendor handles that for you, we're probably not a fit. But if you're open, I can show you how different our approach is in 15 minutes. No pressure. [calendar link]

**Key elements:**
- Acknowledge legitimacy
- Reframe value (research efficiency, not replacing current tool)
- Low-pressure
- Offer proof

---

## 2. Discovery Call Structure (15 min conversion-focused)

**Goal:** Understand their world → Show proof → Close pilot offer

**Open (1 min):**

> "Thanks for making time. Quick context: I help B2B sales teams focus outbound on accounts showing active buying signals. In 15 minutes I want to understand your current bottleneck, show you 2–3 real examples, and if it makes sense, propose a 30-day pilot. Fair?"

**Section A: Current state (3 min)**

Questions (pick 1–2, don't read all):

1. "What's your current process for identifying accounts to reach out to?"
2. "Where's the biggest time sink—research or outreach itself?"
3. "How do you currently decide if someone is a priority?"

*Listen for:* manual research, low reply rate, lack of timing signal.

**Section B: Show proof (5 min)**

Transition:

> "Here's what we're seeing. I pulled 3 accounts in your ICP from this week. Each one triggered a buying signal. Let me show you."

**Then show a real CSV or screenshot with:**
- Company name
- Trigger (funding, hiring, tech switch—one per row)
- Timestamp
- Why it matters (1-line explanation)

*Purpose:* Remove abstraction. Make it concrete.

**Section C: Objection handling (3 min, if needed)**

**If they say:** "But how accurate is this?"

**You say:** "It's not 100%. It's deterministic—if the trigger is real, we surface it. But not every trigger converts. That's exactly why the pilot works: you test on 15–20 accounts and see your own conversion rate."

**If they say:** "This seems expensive"

**You say:** "It's $2,500 for 30 days. Most teams spend that on a single SDR's research time per month. You'll know in 30 days if it's worth more."

**If they say:** "Can you just send me a deck?"

**You say:** "I could, but this is 5 minutes of your time vs. you reading a deck that still won't answer 'does this work for me.' Let's close the pilot conversation now and I'll send all the details after."

---

## 3. Pilot Close Script (closing offer)

**Transition (after proof shown):**

> "Here's what I'm proposing: 30-day pilot. You give me your ICP definition—industry, company size, geography. We surface 15–20 accounts with verified buying signals, generate outreach pack for each one, and you measure results. $2,500. If we don't generate at least 2–3 qualified conversations, next batch is free."

**Then silence. Let them respond.**

**If they ask:** "What if we want to continue?"

**You say:** "We'll have real data on what worked. If it's strong, we scale. If it's not, we refine and try again. Either way, you're only paying for results."

**If they ask:** "When do we start?"

**You say:** "I can have the first batch ready in 7 days. I just need your ICP definition and approval to reach out. I'll send a one-page contract today—takes 5 minutes to sign."

## 4. Contract handoff (non-friction)

**After call, immediately send:**

1. **Contract** (1-pager, see PILOT_CONTRACT_v1.md)
2. **ICP definition form** (simple: industry, size, geography, role)
3. **First batch preview** (3–5 sample accounts from their space, no outreach yet)

**Message:**

> "Great call. Here's the pilot agreement—one page, sign and email back. Also attached: 5 sample accounts we'd include in your batch. You'll see what we mean by 'active signals.' Once you sign, we lock in your ICP and have the full batch ready in 7 days."

## 5. Objection master list (reference)

| Objection | Your response |
|-----------|--------------|
| "Too expensive" | "You spend that on research. 30 days to prove it's not." |
| "Send information" | "10 min call is faster than a deck. See real data." |
| "We already use [vendor]" | "They score leads. We surface active buying windows. Different value." |
| "How accurate?" | "Deterministic on trigger detection. Your conversion rate is what matters." |
| "Can we try for free?" | "No, but next batch is free if this one doesn't generate 2+ meetings." |
| "Need to think about it" | "Fair. ICP definition and contract are ready when you are." |
| "Talk to me in Q3" | "I can put you on the list, but your ICP changes by then. Let's pilot now." |

- [ ] Note their ICP definition (if given)

- [ ] Set a follow-up reminder for 48 hours if no response

## Final principle

**No scripted reading.** Internalize structure, speak naturally. Only read exact transitions and objection responses.

Your credibility comes from sounding conversational + knowledgeable, not polished.
